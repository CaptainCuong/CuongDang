About

I am a research resident at FPT AI Center advised by Prof. Dung D. Le. During my undergraduate years, I also closely collaborated with Prof. Thai Le at University of Mississippi (Ole Miss) and Dr. Hoang Tran at Oak Ridge Lab.

My primary research interests lie in the area of natural language processing (NLP), reinforcement learning (RL), graph neural network (GNN), federated learning (FL) and machine learning (ML). The long-term goal of my research is to enhance the robustness, reliability, privacy, and fairness of NLP systems (e.g. Question Answering and Text Classification), Reinforcement Learning applications (e.g. Autonomous Driving in Adverse Weather Conditions, Traffic Control Systems, and Complex Social Systems), Graph Neural Network applications (e.g. Drug Discovery, Traffic Prediction, Social Network Analysis, Recommender Systems), and Federated Learning algorithms so that they can be reliably used in real-world scenarios. In particular, my current research focuses on i) explaining factors affecting the robustness of NLP models, and ii) building NLP models that are robust to unseen cases in the wild. Here are some keywords:

  • Robust & Reliable Machine Learning
  • Adversarial Attack/Robustness
  • Federated Learning
  • Explainable AI in NLP
  • Graph Neural Network
  • Drug Discovery
