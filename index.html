About

I am currently working as a research associate at KAIST IR&NLP Lab. I obtained my master's degree in the School of Computing from KAIST, where I was advised by Prof. Sung-Hyon Myaeng. My primary research interests lie in the area of natural language processing and machine learning. The long-term goal of my research is to make NLP systems practical so that they can be readily used for each benefit in real-world scenarios. To this end, I am interested in understanding barriers preventing NLP systems from being practical, finding hidden assumptions that are favorable to academic settings, and solving them through novel machine learning approaches. In particular, my research aims to i) make NLP systems cheaper (i.e., efficient) in terms of data and computational resources, and ii) design robust and reliable models to produce desirable outcomes in the wild. Here are some keywords:

  • Question Answering
  • Information Retrieval
  • Efficiency
  • Robustness
Currently applying for Ph.D programs for Fall 2022 in the United States!
